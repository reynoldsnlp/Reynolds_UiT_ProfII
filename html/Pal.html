Softness of a consonant should be preserved

If a consonant is soft, this must be shown by using the correct “soft” vowel letters (see table below) or the soft sign ь

Paired consonants followed by the letters -я, -е, -ё, -и, -ю, -ь are soft

The л in земля 'land' is soft, as we see in forms like Dative/Locative Singular земле, Instrumental Singular землёй, Genitive Singular земли, Accusative Singular землю, Genitive Plural земель

Paired consonants are the consonants that can be either soft or not hard: б, в, д, з, л, м, н, п, р, с, т, ф

Note these correspondences between “hard” vowel letters that indicate that the preceding paired consonant is hard, as opposed to “soft” vowel letters that indicate that the preceding paired consonant is soft

“Hard” vowel letters “Soft” vowel letters
a я
э e
ы и
o ë
у ю

Some “soft stem” adjectives end in -ний and require “soft” vowel letters in all of their endings

An adjective in -ний like последний 'last' has these forms:

Case Masculine and Neuter Feminine Plural
Nominative последний, последнее последняя последние
Genitive последнего последней последних
Dative последнему последней последним
Accusative последний, последнего, последнее последнюю последние, последних
Instrumental последним последней последними
Locative последнем последней последних

Some other examples of soft stem adjectives are внутренний 'internal', ранний 'early', соседний 'neighboring', средний 'central, average', внешний 'external'
