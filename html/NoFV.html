Fill vowel should be added

When two consonants appear at the end of a stem and there is no vowel following, а fill vowel of o or e (sometimes и) may be added

Nouns

Nouns ending in a consonant in the Nominative Singular will have the fill vowel in that form, but delete the fill vowel in all other forms

Nominative Singular -> Genitive singular отец -> отца 'father', день -> дня 'day', сон -> сна 'sleep', платок -> платка 'scarf', церковь -> церкви 'church'

Nouns ending in a consonant in the Genitive Plural will have a fill vowel in that form but delete the fill vowel in all other forms (unless the Genitive Plural ends in a husher followed by -ей)

Genitive Plural -> Nominative Singular девушек -> девушка 'girl', коробок -> коробка 'box', семей -> семья 'family', окон -> окно 'window', житей -> житьё 'life'

Adjectives

Short form adjectives with a fill vowel in the Masculine Singular delete the fill vowel in all other forms

Masculine Singular -> Feminine Singular доволен -> довольна 'satisfied', смешон -> смешна 'funny

The fill vowel и in the Nominative Masculine Singular form of adjectives ending in -ий is dropped in all other forms

Nominative Masculine Singular -> Nominative Feminine Singular собачий -> собачья 'dog's'

Other

Fill vowels occur in the pronouns весь -> вся 'all' and чей -> чья 'whose'

Fill vowels occur in the numerals один -> одна 'one', восемь -> восьми 'eight', сот -> сто 'hundred'

The fill vowel и in Genitive Plural яиц 'eggs' is dropped in all other forms, such as Nominative Singular яйцо

The fill vowel и in Masculine Singular достоин 'worthy' is dropped in all other forms, such as Feminine достойна

Fill vowels can also appear in the prefixes and roots of verbs, where the rules are slightly different
