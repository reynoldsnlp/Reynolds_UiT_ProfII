Spelling rule does not allow ы after certain consonants

After the velar consonants г, к, х and the husher consonants ж, ч, ш, щ we write и, not ы

When we add an ending that would begin with ы to stems ending in г, к, х, ж, ч, ш, щ, we change the ы to и

This rule is important for the following endings where -ы -> -и: Masculine and Feminine nouns in the Nominative and Accusative Singular, and Feminine nouns in the Genitive Singular, Adjectives in Masculine Nominative Singular, Masculine and Neuter Instrumental Singular, and in all Plural endings

Adjectives in Masculine Nominative Singular: русский 'Russian', свежий 'fresh', горячий 'hot', младший 'younger', будущий 'future'

Nouns in Nominative Singular: руки 'arms', ноги 'legs', мухи 'flies', ножи 'knives', врачи 'doctors', карандаши 'pencils', овощи 'vegetables'

When the vowel of the Masculine Nominative Singular adjective ending is stressed, we write -oй: Masculine Nominative Singular большой 'big', дорогой 'dear, expensive'

This is part of a general spelling rule in Russian that also includes this: After the velar consonants г, к, х and the husher consonants ж, ч, ш, щ we also write а, у, not я, ю
