Fleeting vowel should be added

When two consonants appear at the end of a stem and there is no vowel following, а fleeting vowel of o or e (sometimes и) may be added

Nouns

Masculine and Feminine noun stems ending in two consonants will have no fleeting vowel when there are endings, but will add the fleeting vowel in the Nominative Singular

Genitive Singular -> Nominative Singular отца -> отец 'father', дня -> день 'day', сна -> сон 'sleep', платка -> платок 'scarf', церкви -> церковь 'church'

Nouns with zero ending in the Genitive Plural and stems ending in two consonants will have a fleeting vowel in the Genitive Plural that is not present in other forms

Nominative Singular -> Genitive Plural девушка -> девушек 'girl', коробка -> коробок 'box', семья -> семей 'family', окно -> окон 'window'

Adjectives

Short form adjectives with stems ending in two consonants can have a fleeting vowel in the Masculine Singular form that is not present in other forms

Feminine Singular -> Masculine Singular довольна -> доволен 'satisfied', смешна -> смешон 'funny'

The fleeting vowel и in the Nominative Masculine Singular form of adjectives ending in -ий is not present in other forms

Nominative Feminine Singular -> Nominative Masculine Singular собачья -> собачий 'dog's'

Other

Fleeting vowels occur in the Masculine forms of pronouns вся -> весь 'all (Nominative Feminine Singular -> Nominative Masculine Singular)' and чья -> чей 'whose (Nominative Feminine Singular -> Nominative Masculine Singular)'

Fleeting vowels occur in the numerals один -> одна 'one', восемь -> восьми 'eight', сот -> сто 'hundred'

The fleeting vowel и in Genitive Plural яиц 'eggs' is dropped in all other forms, such as Nominative Singular яйцо

The fleeting vowel и in Masculine Singular достоин 'worthy' is dropped in all other forms, such as Feminine достойна

Fleeting vowels can also appear in prepositions and in the prefixes and roots of verbs, as in со студентом 'with the student', подобрать 'pick up', подберу 'I will pick up'
