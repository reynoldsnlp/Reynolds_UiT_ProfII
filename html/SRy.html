Spelling rule does not allow ы after certain consonants

After the velar consonants г, к, х and the husher consonants ж, ч, ш, щ we write и, not ы

When we add an ending that would begin with ы to stems ending in г, к, х, ж, ч, ш, щ, we change the и to ы

This rule is important for the following endings: Masculine and Feminine nouns in the Nominative and Accusative Singular, and Feminine nouns in the Genitive Singular, where -ы -> -и Adjectives in Masculine Nominative Singular, Masculine and Neuter Instrumental Singular, and in all Plural endings, where -ый -> -ий,-ым -> -им, -ыe -> -иe, -ых -> -их, -ыми -> -ими

Adjectives in Masculine Nominative Singular: русский 'Russian', свежий 'fresh', рабочий 'worker', младший 'younger', будущий 'future'

Nouns in Nominative Singular: руки 'arms', ноги 'legs', мухи 'flies', ножи 'knives', врачи 'doctors', карандаши 'pencils', овощи 'vegetables'

When the vowel of the Masculine Nominative Singular adjective ending is stressed, we write -oй: Masculine Nominative Singular большой 'big'

This is part of a general spelling rule in Russian that also includes this: After the velar consonants г, к, х and the husher consonants ж, ч, ш, щ we also write а, у, not я, ю

The letter ц can be followed by both и and ы, but there are rules

In the stems of words, the letter ц is usually followed by the letter и

цирк 'circus', цикл 'cycle', цитат 'citation', цифр 'number, cipher', цивилизация 'civilization', циркуляция 'circulation', цилиндр 'cylinder'

A few stems contain the letter ц followed by ы: цыплёнок 'chick', цыган 'gypsy', на цыпочках 'on tiptoe'

In endings the letter ц is followed by ы

огруцы 'cucumbers', пальцы 'fingers', ножницы 'scissors', больницы 'hospitals'
